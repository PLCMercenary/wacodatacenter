STATE SENATOR FOCUS AREAS:

TEXAS SENATE POWERS:
* Confirm Governor's appointees to state boards/commissions
* Originate major legislation (especially appropriations)
* Conduct investigations and hold hearings
* Provide "advice and consent" on executive actions
* Override vetoes with House (2/3 vote required)

SENATE COMMITTEES (Relevant to data center/environmental issues):
- Natural Resources & Economic Development
- Water, Agriculture & Rural Affairs
- Business & Commerce
- Local Government

- Chair or serve on powerful committees
- Request state agency investigations
- Block or advance House bills
- Influence state budget priorities
- Confirm or reject agency appointees
- Hold public hearings on important issues

KEY DIFFERENCES - SENATOR VS. REPRESENTATIVE:

STATE SENATOR:
- Longer terms (4 years vs. 2 years)
- Fewer members (31 vs. 150) = more influence per member
- Confirms gubernatorial appointments
- Traditionally handles "bigger picture" legislation
- More institutional power and seniority weight

STATE REPRESENTATIVE:
- More members = more specialized knowledge
- Bills often originate here first
- More frequent elections = more responsive to constituents
- Handle more local/specific issues

STRATEGY: Contact both on major issues, but emphasize statewide significance with senators.

TIMING AND STRATEGY:

WHEN TO CONTACT:
- Before session: Build relationship, preview issues
- During session: Urge action on specific bills
- Interim: Request committee hearings or studies
- Before appointee votes: Express support/opposition

FOLLOW-UP:
* Ask for written response to your letter
* Request meeting with senator or staff
* Offer to testify at committee hearings
* Join constituent advisory groups if offered
* Track bills and votes on your issue
* Thank senator for supportive votes

MAXIMIZE IMPACT:
- Coordinate with other constituents (multiple letters matter)
- Provide specific bill language if you want legislation
- Bring local experts (engineers, farmers, business owners)
- Cite economic impact (jobs, tax revenue, costs)
- Show bipartisan constituent support
