COMMON FOLLOW-UP SCENARIOS:

IF YOU RECEIVE A COST ESTIMATE:
- Review it for reasonableness (typically $0.10 per page)
- Respond within time frame given
- Can ask for records to be emailed (lower cost)
- Can narrow request to reduce fees

IF YOU RECEIVE PARTIAL DENIAL:
- Review the cited exemption
- Request a copy of the AG opinion request
- File a complaint with AG if denial seems improper
- Consider narrowing request to get non-exempt portions

IF YOU RECEIVE NO RESPONSE IN 10 DAYS:
- Send follow-up email/letter
- File complaint with Attorney General
- Contact local media if pattern of non-compliance

IMPORTANT TEXAS PIA INFORMATION:

TIMELINE:
- 10 business days for initial response
- Extensions possible for voluminous requests
- Must provide records "promptly" after payment

FEES:
- Copying: typically $0.10 per page
- Labor: $15/hour for locating records (if over 50 pages)
- No charge if under one hour of labor
- No charge for first 50 pages
- Elected officials can charge less or waive fees

EXEMPTIONS THAT MAY APPLY:
- Executive session discussions (limited)
- Pending litigation
- Attorney-client communications
- Personnel matters
- Competitive bidding (temporarily)

CANNOT BE DENIED FOR:
- Being "too burdensome" (must provide cost estimate)
- Not having "valid reason" (no need to explain why)
- Being from out of state or "outsider"

===================================================================

TIPS FOR SUCCESSFUL REQUESTS:

* Be specific about date ranges and topics
* Start narrow - you can always expand
* Request email delivery (faster and cheaper)
* Keep copy of your request
* Document all communications
* Follow up if no response in 10 days
* Be polite but persistent
* Know your rights under the PIA

- Don't request "all records about..." (too broad)
- Don't make demands or threaten
- Don't pay without seeing cost estimate first
- Don't accept "we don't have that" without verification

===================================================================

WHAT MINUTES SHOULD CONTAIN:

Texas law requires meeting minutes to include:
* Date, time, and location of meeting
* Members present and absent
* Approval of previous minutes
* Summary of subjects discussed
* Record of votes taken (who voted how)
* Action items and decisions
* Executive session notation (topic only, not discussion)

Minutes do NOT include:
- Verbatim transcript (unless body chooses to)
- Names of all public speakers (unless specifically noted)
- Full discussion details (summary only)
